Abstract

Robot-assisted minimally invasive surgery has revolutionized surgical practices, and its automation has been a long-time standing goal for research. However, existing surgical research platforms like the dVRK and Raven are limited worldwide and suffer from poor precision due to inaccurate joint measurements. To address these challenges and enhance the accessibility of surgical robotics, we propose a modular surgical research platform (MSR) that integrates a general robot arm with a custom-designed 5-DoF modular adaptor. The adaptor can drive both instruments and the laparoscope, and features a quick-release structure for convenient assembly. Besides, it can be flexibly combined for multi-arm operations. To control the MSR in real-time under critical safety constraints, we formulate our control tasks as a unified quadratic programming (QP) problem which can be solved efficiently. This approach ensures safe and precise operations under joint limits, joint failures and dynamic RCM scenarios. Our MSR can achieve the sub-millimeter positional precision of 0.75mm. We further develop advanced tasks to facilitate diverse human-machine interactions, including intuitive teleoperation and its potential for performing hand-hand or hand-eye collaborative and fully automated surgical tasks. Extensive experiments validate the MSR's versatility for safe operations in confined space and for the first time show its robustness under certain joint failures, which surpass current surgical research platforms.

Highlights

  1. Enhanced tip positional accuracy@0.75 mm superior to the dVRK system, which facilitates surgical autonomy.
  2. The proposed safety-constraint controller can handle typical surgical safety scenarios simultaneously in real-time@60fps.
  3. The proposed MSR platform is compatible with the dVRK platform for executing same dexterous operations, allowing the surgical robotics community to focus on policy learning with seamless cross-platform deployment;
  4. The modular adaptor allows easy replacement of instruments or laparoscope with a quick-release structure and it can be attached to other general robot arms, such as KUKA, FranKa, etc.

Human-machine-interaction Tasks

1. Intuitive manual operation

Using tele-operation device, we can intuitively control the instruments to perform dexterous manipulation tasks.

Peg transfer

It involves moving blocks from one peg to another, requiring operator to grasp, lift and place the block into the target peg.

Pattern cutting

This task requires bimanual operations that the LND holds the object and the MCS performs the cutting.

Bimanual knot tying

A fundamental surgical procedure used for tissue approximation or blood vessels ligation.

Bimanual suturing

A standard surgical procedure usually performed at the end of laparoscopy surgery to close incisions or wounds.

2. Cooperative operation

The MSR system can be operated in a collaborative mode, where one arm is manually teleoperated to conduct complex manual operations, while the other is automatically controlled to assist operators.

Hand-eye collaboration

Collaborative FoV: FoV (left) is automatically controlled to assist manually controlled LND (right) in performing large-scale peg-transfer task.

Hand-hand collaboration

Collaborative instrument: LND1 (left) is automatically controlled to assist manully controlled LND2 (right) in performing bi-manual needle transfer task.
